Air Transport to Eilat

Eilat sees many flights come in per day through three separate airports: Eilat Airport, Ramon Airport, and Ovda Airport.

Eilat Airport is a small airport close to the center of the city, with one small runway and ramp space for only two aircraft. The airport handles mostly regional flights from other airports in Israel, with Ovda Airport handling most international flights. Ovda is technically a military airbase, but it handles civilian air traffic since larger aircraft on international flights can't land on Eilat's runway.

Ramon Airport was built to address various concerns, such as opening land up in Eilat for further development, decreasing reliance on Ovda Airport, handling more air traffic, and so on. The airport opened January 2019 and can handle 2 million passengers per year (~66% of Eilat's yearly tourist traffic) but will later be expanded to accommodate 4 million/year. Eilat Airport is expected to shut down March 2019, and Ovda is expected to stop handling civilian traffic.
